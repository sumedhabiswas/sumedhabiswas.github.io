About

Biography & Profile

My research sits at the intersection of gravitational wave physics and time-domain astronomy. I build the observational infrastructure to find electromagnetic counterparts of compact binary mergers — designing follow-up strategies for wide-field surveys like Gaia and BlackGEM, developing statistical methods to identify the host galaxies of binary mergers, and turning those identifications into constraints on the Hubble constant.

I helped commission the BlackGEM telescope array at ESO La Silla, Chile, and have been a member of the LIGO–Virgo–KAGRA Collaboration since 2019. My work also spans ESA Gaia DPAC, the BlackGEM Consortium, and ENGRAVE.

Outside astrophysics, I am working toward a move into space systems. The toolkit of GW data analysis — Bayesian inference, signal processing at the noise floor, large-scale simulation, HPC pipelines — is directly applicable to problems in mission analysis, GNC, and space instrumentation, and I am building hands-on astrodynamics experience to complement it.

Projects

Research · Aerospace
PhD Research
Radboud University · 2020–2026
I

EM Counterparts of GW Events

Designing observing strategies with Gaia (GaiaX) and MeerLICHT/BlackGEM to detect kilonovae and afterglows from LVK binary mergers during O4. Characterised 11,000+ GaiaX transient candidates ahead of O4.

II

Dark Sirens & the Hubble Constant

Performance verification for determining H₀ and identifying host galaxies of GW events using WEAVE spectroscopic data. Bayesian statistical framework for dark standard sirens with LVK O4 binary black hole events.

III

Fast X-ray Transients & Magnetars

Comparing the volumetric space densities of millisecond-spin magnetars and fast X-ray transients to probe evolutionary channels and common-envelope physics.

IV

Fast X-ray Transients & Dark GRBs

Exploring the connection between fast X-ray transients and dark gamma-ray bursts, probing the nature of the progenitor population and the role of dust extinction in obscured transients.

V

Host Galaxies with 3G Detectors

Determining the host galaxies of binary black hole mergers using the dramatically improved sky localisation from third-generation GW detector networks (Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer).

Masters Research
Nikhef, Amsterdam · 2019–2020
I

Primordial Black Holes in the Lower Mass Gap

Distinguishing primordial and astrophysical binary black holes in the lower mass gap using GW parameter estimation, probing dark matter in the form of sub-solar mass compact objects.

II

Sub-Solar Mass GW Searches

Improving matched-filter search pipelines for sub-solar mass primordial black holes in LIGO/Virgo data, including template bank design and sensitivity characterisation.

Bachelor Projects
2017–2018
I

Binary & White Dwarf Merger Simulations

Used numerical relativity tools to determine general solutions of binary black hole and white dwarf mergers. International Centre for Theoretical Sciences (ICTS), Bangalore, Feb–Aug 2018.

II

Three-Body Problem Dynamics

Two-body dynamics and simulation of special solutions to the general three-body problem, including restricted and figure-eight solutions. St. Xavier's College, Kolkata, Apr–May 2017.
