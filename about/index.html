My research sits at the intersection of gravitational wave physics and time-domain astronomy. I build the observational infrastructure to find electromagnetic counterparts of compact binary mergers — designing follow-up strategies for wide-field surveys like Gaia and BlackGEM, developing statistical methods to identify the host galaxies of binary mergers, and turning those identifications into constraints on the Hubble constant.

I helped commission the BlackGEM telescope array at ESO La Silla, Chile, and have been a member of the LIGO–Virgo–KAGRA Collaboration since 2019. My work also spans ESA Gaia DPAC, the BlackGEM Consortium, and ENGRAVE.

Outside astrophysics, I am working toward a move into space systems. The toolkit of GW data analysis — Bayesian inference, signal processing at the noise floor, large-scale simulation, HPC pipelines — is directly applicable to problems in mission analysis, GNC, and space instrumentation, and I am building hands-on astrodynamics experience to complement it.
